Senior Capstone Experience Reflection

The Learning Process

This project represents the culmination of my technical development within the Cyber Security track. The process began with identifying a critical infrastructure gap on Oahu: the fragmentation of broadband data. From the initial proposal to the final synthesis, the project required a shift from theoretical research to the practical application of Geographic Information Systems (GIS). Navigating this entire process taught me that infrastructure resilience is a necessity for community safety and digital equity.

The Learning Stretch

The primary "learning stretch" for this project involved mastering QGIS software. I navigated a difficult learning curve while attempting to georeference raw data points and overlay complex hazard maps. One major setback occurred due to the lack of available fiber data from private providers, which required me to pivot my methodology to focus on a "proof of concept" using public nodes.

Overcoming these inconsistencies in public geospatial data allowed me to move beyond simple mapping and into the realm of network engineering. Another setback that I couldn’t overcome was not using ArcGIS. As a paid software, it offers simpler layering mechanics that may have mitigated my time constraints, but utilizing the open-source QGIS forced a deeper understanding of the technical process.

The Importance and Influence of STEM

STEM principles are the foundation of modern societal resilience. Throughout this project, I applied engineering design principles to model connectivity and utilized mathematical ratios to quantify physical risk. By calculating an Infrastructure Risk Ratio (IRR) of 0.50, I demonstrated that 50% of the surveyed high-speed hubs are vulnerable to environmental hazards. This application of science and technology highlights how data-driven decisions are essential for protecting underserved communities.

Future Plans

While this project is complete for the Capstone requirements, the research establishes a foundation for future work. I intend to expand this model by incorporating 3D topographic data to better simulate environmental stressors like wind and heavy rain. Additionally, I hope to explore automation tools that could transition this static 2D model into a system capable of providing real-time infrastructure updates.

Final STEM Capstone Product

The final product is a comprehensive 2D network map of Oahu that overlays broadband fiber nodes with tsunami inundation zones. The analysis identified an Infrastructure Risk Ratio (IRR) of 0.50, meaning 50% of the surveyed high-speed hubs are located in high-risk areas.
